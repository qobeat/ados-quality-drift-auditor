## Status

Accepted.

## Context

Both v1.4 and v1.5 used `simple-calculator.zip` as a Reflection or runtime proof. Its arithmetic behavior was useful, but it did not test glossary semantics, schema strength, evidence integrity, inventories, ADR references, or the origin of the final proof. The larger failure mechanism was over-generalization combined with a self-owned validator.

## Decision

1. A proof fixture establishes only mapped claims directly exercised by its oracle.
2. Fixture PASS never implies release PASS.
3. Required runtime proofs start from the final artifact or clean extraction.
4. High-risk release claims require isolated mutation tests with exact expected finding codes.
5. A builder-owned validator is one evidence source and is not the sole release oracle.
6. Hard gates override average scores.
7. Failed iterations and raw outputs are retained.

## Consequences

- `simple-calculator.zip` may remain a minimal fixture but cannot be the primary project-quality oracle.
- Replay order and proof origin become verifiable conditions.
- Mutation suites must be designed before or independently of implementation.
- The final verdict may be FAIL despite high structural scores.
