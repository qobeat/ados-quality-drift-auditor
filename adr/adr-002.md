## Status

Accepted.

## Context

The reviewed v1.4 and v1.5 builds showed that PASS records can exist without observations sufficient to support them. Bulk evidence counts obscured missing commands, expected results, observed results, and provenance.

## Decision

1. Every source is decomposed into Atomic Claims before scoring.
2. Claim IDs remain stable through weighting, scoring, comparison, and verdict.
3. Evidence records contain observations and provenance; they do not contain evaluator scores.
4. Scores and verdicts cite claims and evidence.
5. Unsupported decisive propositions use `I cannot confirm this.`
6. Builder-authored evidence is labeled by independence level.

## Consequences

- Evaluation is auditable at claim level.
- Assertion-only records cannot establish PASS.
- Evidence can be superseded without renumbering claims.
- Reports are longer but materially more trustworthy.
