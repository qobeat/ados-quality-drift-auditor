# Evidence and Claims

## Separation rule

A claim is what a source asserts. Evidence is what supports or refutes that assertion. A score is evaluator judgment. These are separate records.

## Observation-bearing evidence

Strong verification evidence normally states:

- procedure or command;
- expected result;
- observed result;
- target and version;
- verifier;
- UTC timestamp;
- relevant stdout, stderr, diff, or parsed values;
- artifact identity or hash;
- final result;
- claims supported.

A target path plus `status: pass` is not enough.

## Independence

Independence is evaluated by logic path, not by filename. Two reports generated by the same validator are one logic path.

## Stable IDs

Do not renumber claims to improve presentation. Cross-version comparison depends on stable IDs within the evaluation.

## Unsupported propositions

Record the missing evidence and write: `I cannot confirm this.`
